# arXiv digest — 2026-07-04    (4 core, 2 relevant)

## Core
### One More Time: Revisiting Neural Quantum States from a Reinforcement Learning Perspective
Juan Agustín Duque, Sergio García Heredia, …, Aaron Courville, Anna Dawid  ·  cs.LG  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02292
Why: Reframes NQS optimization as an advantage policy-gradient problem and introduces Proximal Wavefunction Optimization (PWO), a trust-region training method that beats Adam/minSR/SPRING on Ising and J1-J2 spin models and scales to a 1.5B-parameter autoregressive NQS — a direct NQS training-methods contribution.

### Entropy of Non-Abelian Anyons from Slow Quasiparticle Dynamics in Quantum Hall Interferometers
Eran Sela, Mitali Banerjee  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02188
Why: Proposes an interferometer-based scheme to extract the characteristic O(1) entropy of non-Abelian anyons (e.g. d=√2 for ν=5/2) from equilibrium charge measurements on an antidot — squarely in the fractional quantum Hall / anyon family.

### The Wigner function for Integer quantum Hall effect
Yao Wang, Qi-Ming Fu, …, Li Yu, Kang Li  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02094
Why: Derives the Wigner quasi-probability function for electrons in the Landau gauge and computes it for the integer quantum Hall effect — a phase-space treatment of the same Landau-level physics central to the user's NQS-in-magnetic-field line of work.

### Many-body benchmarking of DFT local-registry energetics in bilayer InSe
Jeonghwan Ahn, Abdulgani Annaberdiyev, …, Nathaniel P. Stern, Hyeondeok Shin  ·  cond-mat.mtrl-sci  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01682
Why: Diffusion Monte Carlo shows DFT badly underestimates stacking-energy corrugation between near-degenerate registries in bilayer InSe, with direct consequences for the accuracy of moiré/twisted-bilayer models.

## Also relevant
### Disorder-induced superconductivity in graphene
Jannes van Poppelen, Tomas Löthman, Annica M. Black-Schaffer  ·  cond-mat.supr-con  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02267
Why: Shows disorder alone (vacancies/hydrogenation) can induce s-wave and mixed d-wave superconductivity in monolayer graphene via a geometric superfluid weight — unconventional 2D superconductivity physics, without an NQS/ML angle.

### Structured Factorization Approaches for Quantum State Tomography
Zhen Qin, Joseph M. Lukens, Brian T. Kirby, Zhihui Zhu  ·  quant-ph  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01608
Why: Unifies tomography priors — low-rank, matrix-product, and neural density operators (MLP/transformer) — under one factorized optimization framework, adjacent to NQS/tensor-network representations of quantum states.
