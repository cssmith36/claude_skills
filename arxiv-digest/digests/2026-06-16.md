### Compact Spin-Charge Separated Neural Quantum States for Valence-Bond States
Ang-Kun Wu, Louis Primeau, …, Adrian Del Maestro, Yang Zhang  ·  cond-mat.str-el  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17045
Why: New NQS architecture built around four physics-motivated components — solvable-point-guided convolution, geometric pooling, a sign-resolving tanh(x^{2k+1}) activation, and explicit spin-hole sector separation — achieving compact, high-fidelity representations of sign-structured, constrained Hilbert spaces in t-J-like models with ~L² vs ~L⁴ MPS parameter scaling in gapless regimes. Directly in scope as an NQS methods paper and a stepping stone toward physics-informed architectures for the Hubbard family.

## Also relevant

### Optical vortex probe of loop-current chirality in moiré materials
Nobuhiko Yokoshi, Akihito Kato  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16676
Why: Proposes angular-momentum-resolved nonlinear optics (optical vortex beams) to isolate intrinsic loop-current chirality in twisted bilayer graphene via the Δℓ=3 channel of the helicity-dependent dc photocurrent — a new symmetry-resolved probe of geometric chirality in moiré systems.

### Possible high temperature superconductivity induced by coupling to proximate hyperbolic photon modes
Zhiyu Dong, Patrick A. Lee  ·  cond-mat.supr-con  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16674
Why: Identifies an unconventional pairing mechanism in a 2D metal on hBN via virtual exchange of hyperbolic phonon-polariton modes (Anderson-Morel analogue in energy space), estimating very high T_c even at small coupling — unconventional 2D superconductivity without an NQS angle.

### Intervalley coherence and flavor polarization in three-valley moiré systems
Jeyong Park, Laura Classen, Mathias S. Scheurer  ·  cond-mat.str-el  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16622
Why: Classifies interaction-driven instabilities — flavor-polarized and intervalley-coherent orders — in moiré superlattices of twisted M-point materials, revealing richer correlated-phase structure than the well-studied two-valley (graphene) case.

### Preparation of Fractional Quantum Hall States on Quantum Computers
Hao Wu, Lei-Yi-Nan Liu, …, Zhao Liu, Jian Cui  ·  quant-ph  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16548
Why: Direct quantum-circuit construction of the ν=1/3 Laughlin state on arbitrary sphere geometry with reduced two-qubit gate count, plus optimal-control pulses for superconducting and Rydberg platforms — FQH state preparation on quantum computers (calibration: RELEVANT).

### Vortex-core size and quantum geometry in flat-band superconductors
M. Özkurt, M. Iskin  ·  cond-mat.supr-con  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16536
Why: Derives a closed-form coherence length in terms of the quantum metric of Bloch states and confirms via self-consistent BdG that flat-band vortex-core size is governed by a geometric pair mass rather than a BCS Fermi surface — a quantum-geometry mechanism directly relevant to moiré flat-band superconductivity.

### Learning ground state observables from quantum computing experiments
Ben Jaderberg, Freya Shah, …, Christa Zoufal, Kunal Sharma  ·  quant-ph  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15983
Why: Neural networks trained on quantum-hardware data from approximate ground states of the 2D XXZ Heisenberg model (up to 115 qubits) predict spatially resolved observables in- and out-of-distribution — practical demonstration of ML-for-quantum-many-body at scale.

### Machine Learning Topological Order from Defect Partition Functions
Kazem Bitaghsir Fadafan, Wei Cui, Babak Haghighat, Shailesh Lal  ·  cond-mat.dis-nn  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15799
