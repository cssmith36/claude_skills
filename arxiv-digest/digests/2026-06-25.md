## Core

### Probing the pairing symmetry of moiré graphene superconductors
Sayak Biswas, Rajdeep Sensarma, Mohit Randeria  ·  cond-mat.supr-con  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23994
Why: Constrains superconducting order parameters emerging from the incommensurate Kekulé spiral (IKS) normal state of magic-angle moiré graphene, then proposes spectroscopic and phase-sensitive (Andreev bound state) tests to distinguish singlet vs. triplet pairing — directly targeting the pairing symmetry of moiré superconductivity.

### Observation of even-denominator fractional quantum Hall states at ν=3/4 and 5/4 in the lowest Landau level
Siddharth Kumar Singh, Chengyu Wang, …, Loren N. Pfeiffer, Mansour Shayegan  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23990
Why: Reports experimental discovery of new even-denominator FQH ground states in a wide GaAs quantum well, described via the Halperin Ψ₃₃₁ state and bilayer composite fermions — new exotic FQH ground states in the lowest Landau level directly in the family targeted by NQS-for-QH work.

## Also relevant

### Anyon Exchange Phase from Antidot Interferometry
Matthias Thamm, Felix Puster, Bernd Rosenow  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24831
Why: Keldysh theory of a Fabry-Perot interferometer with a quantum antidot shows non-monotonic transmission phase that allows extraction of the bare anyon exchange phase — FQH quasiparticle interferometry physics adjacent to the user's NQS-for-QH line.

Why: Comprehensive Les Houches lecture notes covering MPS, DMRG, time evolution, PEPS, and open-system (Lindblad) tensor networks with ITensor/Julia code — a reference resource at the NQS ↔ tensor-network interplay.

### Simulating the Haldane model in ultra-clean GaAs heterostructures
Francesco Cioni, Lorenzo Cavicchi, Fabio Taddei, Marco Polini  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24670
Why: Proposes realizing the Haldane Chern insulator in a high-mobility GaAs 2DEG with a gate-defined honeycomb potential and patterned ferromagnetic field — topological physics in the artificial-graphene / 2DEG platform the user works on.

### Enhancing quantum-classical configuration interaction methods using a neural-network classifier
Severino Zeni, Giovanni Varutti, Jacopo Nespolo, Dimitrios Trypogeorgos  ·  physics.chem-ph  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24332
Why: Integrates an NN classifier into the iterative CI loop via active learning to guide determinant selection, achieving ~5× memory/cost reduction for classical HCI and faster convergence for quantum-classical SQD-CI — ML-guided compression of variational spaces for quantum chemistry.
