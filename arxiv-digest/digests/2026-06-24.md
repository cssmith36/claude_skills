# arXiv digest — 2026-06-24    (3 core, 4 relevant)

## Core

### Resolving support-mismatch by local basis rotation in variational Monte Carlo
Jia-Lin Chen, Zhen Fan, …, Yantao Wu, Tao Xiang  ·  cond-mat.str-el  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23657
Why: Introduces a local basis-rotation sampling scheme that resolves the exponential configuration suppression after a charged-operator projection, enabling unbiased VMC real-time dynamics in 2D and improving ground-state calculations; directly applicable to NQS-based VMC implementations.

### Helical Domain-Wall-Ring Networks Reshape Superconducting Correlations
Yi-Chun Hung, Arun Bansil  ·  cond-mat.mes-hall  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23599
Why: Studies superconducting correlations in helical domain-wall-ring networks formed within moiré patterns of a twisted bilayer honeycomb lattice, finding that inter-ring pair tunneling is RG-relevant but strongly suppressed by ring-geometry finite-size effects — a qualitative departure from infinite-system expectations.

### Kohn-Luttinger like superconductivity in twisted bilayer graphene at large twist angles
A. O. Sboychakov  ·  cond-mat.supr-con  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23308
Why: Predicts d-wave superconductivity in large-twist-angle TBG driven by screened Coulomb interactions via a Kohn-Luttinger mechanism, with T_c reaching hundreds of millikelvin tunable by bias voltage.

## Also relevant

### Dissipative preparation of Laughlin-like states
Tong Liu  ·  quant-ph  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23451
Why: Proposes a purely dissipative Lindbladian protocol (local loss and pump channels) that targets Laughlin-like FQH states at filling 1/3 as exact steady states, with a gap bounded from below and demonstrated adiabatic pumping on near-term quantum simulators.

### Harnessing Josephson-Shapiro physics to verify interlayer exciton superfluidity
Filippo Pascucci, Alexander R. Hamilton, Milorad V. Milosevic, David Neilson  ·  cond-mat.supr-con  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23303
Why: Predicts resolvable Shapiro plateaus in a Dayem-bridge excitonic Josephson junction in double-bilayer graphene and TMDs, with a nonmonotonic BCS-BEC crossover signature tied to the healing length — a direct probe of exciton superfluidity in 2D correlated electron-hole systems.

### Scalable Physics-Inspired Transformers for Spin Glasses
Lu Zhong, Wenli Duan, …, Pan Zhang, Ying Tang  ·  cond-mat.dis-nn  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22984
Why: Physics-inspired transformer with sparse attention and FlashAttention-parallelised ancestral sampling achieves up to two orders of magnitude speedup over standard variational autoregressive networks for Boltzmann sampling in SK and 2D/3D Edwards-Anderson spin glasses, scaling neural-network simulation to unprecedented system sizes.

### An interpretable closed form for entanglement entropy from bitstrings, guided by a graph neural network
Anas Saleh  ·  quant-ph  ·  https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22713
Why: A GNN trained on Rydberg-array bitstrings identifies boundary two-point correlators as the key features, from which an exhaustive search yields a six-term closed-form approximation for bipartite von Neumann entropy; validated by DMRG up to 100 atoms.
